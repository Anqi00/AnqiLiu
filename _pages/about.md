# Welcome

I am **Anqi Liu**, a Ph.D. student at **Chonnam National University**, working at the intersection of **robotics, computer vision, and intelligent agriculture**. 

My research focuses on developing robust perception and decision-making systems for orchard robots. I am particularly interested in leveraging advanced 3D vision and deep learning techniques to solve complex agricultural automation challenges. 

**Core Research Interests:**
* 3D Point Cloud Processing & Reconstruction (3DGS, SfM)
* Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) for Structural Analysis
* Precision Pruning & Agricultural Automation
* 2D–3D Camera Pose Registration
* Vision-Guided Robotic Manipulation 

# Research Overview

Modern orchard robots require reliable perception in unstructured environments, accurate localization, and biologically informed decision-making. My work aims to bridge these components by integrating high-fidelity 3D reconstruction, topology-aware graph learning, and robotic execution into a unified framework.

Currently, my research explores:
* **Topology-Aware Decision Making:** Skeleton-based pruning point prediction using deep graph learning.
* **Advanced Scene Representation:** Optimizing 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) and structure-aware reconstruction for slender, complex orchard environments.
* **Robust Localization & Alignment:** 2D–3D registration between offline reconstructed orchard maps and online camera observations.
* **Robotic Execution:** Visual servoing and manipulation planning within agricultural simulation worlds.

# Selected Projects

### TopoSkel-GNN: Biologically Informed Pruning Prediction
A topology-aware graph learning framework designed to predict optimal pruning points directly on apple tree skeleton graphs, enabling automated branch-level decision making.

### Structure-Aware 3D Reconstruction in Orchards
Developing customized reconstruction pipelines (utilizing techniques like 3DGS and COLMAP) to capture and represent the complex, slender structures of dormant fruit trees from combined aerial (drone) and ground-based imaging.

### Orchard Robotic Manipulation in Simulation Worlds
Implementation of an autonomous perception and manipulation pipeline. This includes visual servoing simulations for robotic arms (e.g., Franka Panda) using MuJoCo and ROS 2 to precisely target pruning locations in virtual agricultural environments.
